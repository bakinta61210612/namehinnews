DaBaby Concert Canceled Due to Low Ticket Sales Following Controversy

August 31, 2024
post

DaBaby‘s concert is not moving forward.

The 30-year-old rapper, who faced much backlash after going on a homophobic rant last year, was set to perform on Friday (September 2) at the Smoothie King Center in New Orleans, La.

The event has since been canceled, and it has to do with low ticket sales, via NOLA.com.

Click inside to read more…

According to the New Orleans outlet, hardly any advance tickets had been sold, even when priced as low as $35. The number of tickets sold appeared to be fewer than 500. The arena has a concert capacity of approximately 14,000.

The show’s promoter seemingly decided to cancel the show entirely, as the page was deactivated on Ticketmaster.

Last year, DaBaby said he was taking steps to learn following his controversial outburst.

In the music video for his latest release “Tough Skin,” he is seen hanging on a cross while being attacked with bricks and a flame thrower, which generated a lot of mixed reactions on social media for his comparisons to being crucified.

Rolling Stone published a new video of DaBaby‘s 2018 shooting earlier this year, saying the footage may call into question his involvement in the fatal incident.
