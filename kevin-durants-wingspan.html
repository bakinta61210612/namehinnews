How Kevin Durants Wingspan Gives Him an Edge on the Court

September 07, 2024
post

In this aricle, we will talk about Kevin Durant’s wingspan and other impressive physical attributes.

Kevin Durant is one of the best basketball players in the world, with a net worth of $300 million and a resume that includes two NBA championships, two Finals MVP awards, and an Olympic gold medal.

But what makes him so dominant on the court? One of the key factors is his incredible wingspan, which is 7-foot-5, six inches longer than his height of 6-foot-10.

What is Wingspan and Why Does It Matter?

Wingspan is the distance between the tips of a person’s fingers when they stretch their arms out to the sides.

It is usually similar to a person’s height, but some people have a disproportionate wingspan that gives them an advantage in certain sports, especially basketball.

A longer wingspan allows a player to reach higher, cover more space, and disrupt opponents more easily.

It also helps with shooting, rebounding, passing, and defending.

For example, Durant can shoot over taller defenders, grab rebounds out of their reach, pass around them, and block or steal their shots.

Kevin Durant has freakish wingspan

Measuring 6 feet 10 inches, Kevin Durant, the forward for the Brooklyn Nets, doesn’t rank among the NBA’s top 50 tallest players.

Surprisingly, his wingspan spans 7 feet 5 inches, placing him in the top 30 for this attribute.

Durant boasts an unusual height-to-wingspan ratio, which contributes to his prowess as one of the finest scorers in the game.

As a two-time champion, he excels as an elite scorer across all three levels and holds his own as a capable defender.

In many ways, Durant embodies the qualities of a shooting guard confined within the body of a center, making him a perfect fit for the positionless era of basketball.

His versatile offensive repertoire enables him to easily dominate opponents.

This four-time scoring champion is undeniably a physical anomaly.

Despite his towering height, he exhibits remarkable speed on the court and poses a significant threat on fastbreaks.

Interestingly, despite his impressive height and wingspan, Kevin Durant has difficulty palming a basketball.

He disclosed this peculiar fact during his tenure with the OKC Thunder, stating, “To be honest, I don’t know why this is a story, but yeah, I can’t palm a ball.”

Durant was capable of palming balls during his college and high school days, but he asserts that they were not fully inflated.

It’s worth noting that Durant is not the sole legendary player who struggled to palm an NBA-sized ball, as other greats like Charles Barkley faced similar challenges.

How Does Durant Compare to Other NBA Stars?

Durant’s wingspan is not the longest in the NBA, but it is among the top 30.

Some of the players who have a longer wingspan than him are Rudy Gobert (7-foot-9), Giannis Antetokounmpo (7-foot-3), and Kawhi Leonard (7-foot-3).

However, Durant has a better shooting touch and versatility than most of them, which makes him more lethal on offense.

On the other hand, some of the players who have a shorter wingspan than Durant are LeBron James (7-foot-0), Stephen Curry (6-foot-3), and James Harden (6-foot-10).

These players are also very skilled and talented, but they may struggle more against Durant’s length and reach on defense.

Conclusion

Kevin Durant’s wingspan is one of his greatest assets as a basketball player.

It gives him an edge over his opponents in many aspects of the game.

He is able to use his long arms to score, rebound, pass, and defend at a high level.

He is also able to adapt to different positions and roles on the court, making him a versatile and valuable player for any team.
